<a href="index.html">Yuqun Zhang</a>'s main research interests lie in Software Engineering, and its synergy with Machine Learning and Programming Languages. He has published over 50 research papers, winning two <a href="https://www.sigsoft.org/awards/distinguishedPaperAward.html">ACM SIGSOFT Distinguished Paper Awards</a> (ISSTA 2019 & ICSE 2025). His research has helped detect hundreds of bugs for open-source projects from <a href="https://www.apache.org/">Apache</a> and <a href="https://github.com/">GitHub</a>, as well as  commercial products from <a href="https://intl.alipay.com/">Alipay</a> and <a href="https://www.kuaishou.com//">Kwai</a> where his work on fuzzing, taint analysis, and program analysis has been successfully deployed with million lines of code and over 1 billion global users. He has also been extensively working on developing theories and techniques for artificial intelligence, e.g., Large Language Models, and applying them on software engineering research</a>.

 <h2>Biography</h2>


<p><a href="index.html">Yuqun Zhang</a> is a Tenured Associate Professor in the <a href="https://cse.sustech.edu.cn/">Department of Computer Science and Engineering</a> at <a href="https://www.sustech.edu.cn/">Southern University of Science and Technology</a>. Dr. Zhang got his B.S. in Communication Engineering from <a 
href="http://www.tju.edu.cn/">Tianjin University</a> (China), and M.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the <a 
href="http://www.rochester.edu.">University of Rochester </a> with <a 
href="https://hajim.rochester.edu/ece/heinzelman/">Prof. Wendi Heinzelman</a>. He got his Ph.D. from the <a href="http://www.ece.utexas.edu">Electrical & 
Computer Engineering Department</a> at <a href="http://www.utexas.edu">The 
University of Texas at Austin</a>, where he was very fortunate to work under the supervision of <a 
href="http://users.ece.utexas.edu/~perry/">Prof. Dewayne Perry</a>.

<h2>Research Projects</h2>
<p>I am passionate about building practical software testing, analysis, and debugging systems to predict, detect, diagnose, and fix bugs for all kinds of software systems.<br> Here are some <b>recent fun projects/problems</b> that we have worked on:</p>
<ul> <li><b>Fuzzing</b> [<a href="publications/icse2020a.pdf">ICSE'20</a>,<a href="publications/icse2022a.pdf">ICSE'22a</a>,<a href="publications/icse2022b.pdf">ICSE'22b</a>,<a href="publications/icse2023a.pdf">ICSE'23a</a>,<a href="publications/icse2023b.pdf">ICSE'23b</a>,<a href="publications/fse2023a.pdf">ICSE'23a</a>,<a href="publications/fse2023b.pdf">FSE'23b</a>]: how can we automatically generate structured tests for exposing program bugs in real-world projects? </li>
  <li><b>Other Software Security topics</b> [<a href="publications/issta2023a.pdf">ISSTA'23a</a>,<a href="publications/icse2023c.pdf">ICSE'23c</a>,<a href="publications/icse2024.pdf">ICSE'24</a>]: how can we develop a set of techniques to enhance software security?</li>
<li><b>Automated Program Transformation/Repair</b> [<a href="publications/ase2019.pdf">ASE'19</a>]: how can we automatically infer potential software patches and efficiently explore them to fix real-world bugs?</li>
<li><b>Practical Failure Prediction/Diagnosis</b> [<a href="publications/issta2019.pdf">ISSTA'19</a>, <a href="publications/tse2019.pdf">TSE'19</a>, <a href="publications/issta2021.pdf">ISSTA'21</a>]: how can we predict program defect or precisely identify the root causes for buggy software systems in production?
<li> <b>AI for SE</b> [<a href="publications/tse2020.pdf">TSE'20</a>,<a href="publications/issta2022.pdf">ISSTA'22</a>]: how can we leverage state-of-the-art machine learning/deep learning models for software engineering tasks?</li>
<li> <b>SE for AI</b> [<a href="publications/icse2020b.pdf">ICSE'20b</a>, <a href="publications/ase2018.pdf">ASE'18</a>]: how can we design advanced testing and debugging techniques to detect, localize, and fix bugs for DNN models, libraries, frameworks, and even platform supports?</li>
</ul>
